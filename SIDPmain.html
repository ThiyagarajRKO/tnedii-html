School Innovation Development Project (SIDP)

  • To cultivate talented entrepreneurs in Tamil Nadu, the Entrepreneurship Development and Innovation Institute (EDII) has partnered with the School Education Department. This collaboration aims to transform students' innovative ideas into prototype and support their commercialization, shaping them into entrepreneurs.


  • The Honorable Chief Minister of Tamil Nadu launched the School Innovation Development Program in 2022 at Madurai. The program's primary goal is to enable school students to provide technological solutions to both local and global problems.

  • UNICEF will act as the knowledge partner for implementing the School Innovation Development Project, providing support for all activities, including the E-learning platform.

  • To ensure the success of this project, District Education Officers and all officials within the School Education Department will play a vital role, with the Chief Educational Officer acting as the responsible officer for the School Innovation Development Program.

  • In the current financial year 8,063 government and government-aided high and higher secondary schools are involved into this SIDP Project. To support the registered students in this program, school teachers will be appointed as guide teachers by the headmasters. Selected guide teachers will receive a one-day SIDP orientation training conducted by EDII Master Trainers at the respective district's Chief Educational Office. Afterward, guide teachers must raise awareness about this project among all students and teachers in their schools. Interested students from grades 6 to 12 should form student teams consisting of 2 to 5 members.


  • With the guidance of their Guide teachers, student teams will learn through online animated videos provided by UNICEF and submit their ideas along with the Guide Teachers approval. As an incentive, certificates provided by EDII, students can be downloaded from the Upshift Portal.

  • From the submitted ideas on the online platform, 400 top innovative ideas will be selected by the evaluators.

  • To develop selected 400 ideas into prototypes, EDII will conduct boot camps in the respective districts in coordination with the District Collector. EDII-TN will provide financial assistance and mentor support from the industry experts and the entrepreneurs to 400 student teams. Students will present their prototypes before a jury and 40 teams will be selected to the state level.

  • Selected 40 team will participate in the Final Pitch event at the Entrepreneurship Development and Innovation Institute. Out of the 40 ideas, the top 10 teams will receive ₹1 lakh cash award and certificates, while the remaining 30 teams will receive ₹25,000 cash award and certificates, awarded by the Honorable Minister for Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises.

Achievements for 2023-24

Through the School Innovation Development Project, 755,000 students from grades 9 to 12 in 4,483 government and government-aided higher secondary schools across 38 districts have been benefited. using online Platform, 133,504 students and 5,277 teachers successfully completed the learning module.

A total of 29,342 teams were formed with interested students, and learning activities were conducted online. The student teams collectively submitted 26,130 innovative inventions.

Out of these, 371 teams were evaluated in prototype development camps, and 40 teams have been selected to participate at the state level, with prizes ranging from ₹25,000 to ₹1,00,000.
